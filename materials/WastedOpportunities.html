Here is a list of books that I’ve read (mostly as a child) that would’ve been great to adapt into movies or series.

I won’t specify whether I think these should be animated or live-action, though I am biased in favor of animated stuff 😋

  • The Percy Jackson Series by Rick Riordan
  • Technically, this was adapted, but it was almost as bad as the Avatar: The Last Airbender movie.
  • Good adaptation medium would be a series. Way too much happens in these books to fit into some movies. Maybe just 6-12 episodes a season. Stories from “complementary” books like the Demigod Diaries can be turned into specials or extras.
  • The Kane Chronicles by Rick Riordan
  • After the failed Percy Jackson adaptation, no one touched any of Riordan’s other books, sadly
  • Would be great for a series adaptation as well. It’d probably be shorter, though, because there are only three books instead of five.
  • The Heroes of Olympus by Rick Riordan
  • Look… I’ve read most of Riordan's books, so…
  • This could be either it’s own series or a continuation of the Percy Jackson series since the last book of Percy Jackson and the first book of Heroes of Olympus take place less than a year apart.
  • If this and Percy Jackson were merged into the same series, they can’t call the show Percy Jackson. That would get confusing after the fifth season.
  • Or they could, and just treat the series like the Avatar series “The Last Airbender” and “The Legend of Korra.”
  • The Underland Chronicles by Susan Collins
  • Honestly, I’m a little offended they never tried adapting this book series. It’s just as entertaining as Hunger Games, in my opinion. Just a slightly younger audience.
  • Anyway, it would be really good for a series of movies or a miniseries like Infinity Train. I haven’t read it in a while and don’t exactly remember how much was going on, but I don’t think it would be enough content for a full series, unless they made the seasons short like She-Ra (2018) or Invincible for each book.
  • Seven Realms Novels by Cinda Williams Chima
  • I think people (probably teenegers) that liked Game of Thrones would also find this entertaining. It’s a little… well a lot different, but the medieval-ish setting and royal politics are similar.
  • Anyway, this would definitely be a series. The books average 500 pages each and something is always happening. The first book is admittedly more of a character introduction (think of the season 1 of MHA), but there’s still plot relevant stuff going on.
  • Plus, there’s the sequel series Shattered Realms to milk too.
  • The Darkest Minds by Alexandra Bracken
  • This shit was so unsettling.
  • It’d be in the same vein as Hunger Games and The 5th Wave.
  • would be great as a movie on it’s own.
  • Ok, so I now know (as of 1/2/2022) that there was a movie for it. I had forgotten about it because I saw the trailer and was like “NOPE.” Haven’t watched it yet, so maybe the trailer was just bad. That happens a lot, actually…
  • It could also work as a series if the sequel books are considered, but I haven’t read those yet, so I don’t really know.
  • Fell by David Clement-Davies
  • This book was weird and mystical and I loved it for that. It has a medieval setting, a magical bond between a girl and a wolf (strictly platonic, begone Team Jacob), and just some random-ish events. I don’t really remember much - I read it a long time ago - but I finished it and if I don’t like a book I won’t finish.
  • Although, I do want to re-read it for vetting purposes. It wasn’t long ago that I would’ve recommended Monument 14… ew.
  • I think it would do best as a movie or miniseries at most.
  • The Big Splash by Jack D. Ferraiolo
  • This book was a hilarious Middle School Noire!
  • Definitely would cleanse the palettes of anyone that watched Disney’s Wimpy Kid movie reboot
