Introduction

This document was created to help me plot out the panels for the scripting. All four books will have their own sections in this document. Outlines will be written for each chapter and include plot points, dialogue, setting descriptions, and character descriptions (when necessary).

There will also be a section to record a glossary of important items, settings, and words at the end.

Can find some helpful information here.

Design Details:

  • Odd-numbered pages are on the right side, and even-numbered pages are on the left side.
  • The comic will be colored in simple, desaturated/pale tones with cell shading.
  • Impactful scenes (emotional, character, plot) should be given more weight. Try making the climax of those scenes a full page panel.
  • Flashbacks will be monotone with a misty panel border similar to watercolor to stand out. The flashbacks can also be drawn more simply (like stick figure-esque).

The Demon King

Book One

Chapter One: The Hunt

  • (pages 1-4 of  the scripting were already written before I started outlining)
  • Han and dancer go east to Burnt tree meadow
  • Dancer is going fast; Han begins to relax as they walk
  • spring (blossoms, soft earth, moist grass, etc.) all around
  • Han takes off deerskin jacket and rolls up sleeves later
  • they get to the meadow and find a dozen fellsdeer (large ears, red hides, brown eyes, white tails) grazing.
  • They each take position downwind
  • Han, eager to take down a deer, while Dancer is more call and calculating which deer to shoot.
  • Han aims, but smells smoke on the wind
  • he and dancer exchange worried looks
  • They see orange, purple, and green flames on the crest of the hill
  • the air thickens with smoke
  • the deer run away, directly towards them. they each try to take a shot and miss.
  • Han attempts to sprint after the deer while they are disappearing into the pine trees.
  • The flames rush downhill
  • Dancer, shaking his head: “What is going on? There’s no burns this time of year”
  • the flames spill downhill even faster, leaping small ravines. Glittering embers fall to either side of the flood of fire, but strangely do not spread it.
  • Han and Dancer are squinting now from the heat and have ash in their hair and sparks on their clothes
  • Han: “Come on. We’d better get out of the way!”
  • they run across the ridge, slipping and sliding on wet shale and leaves (a fall is deadly, so they must be high up).
  • They take shelter behind a “rocky prominence piecing the vegetative skin of the mountain”
  • rabbits, foxes, and other small animals run past them and the firey flood is right on their tails
  • the flames pass them by
  • Three riders follow right behind the flames
  • Han stares, mesmerized
  • the three boys wore fine cloaks of silk and summer wool long enough to touch their stirrups, as well as glittering stoles with fancy emblems. They rode flatlander horses with long delicate legs and proudly arched necks. saddles and bridles have silver fittings
  • boys ride with loose and easy arrogance, as if oblivious to breathtaking landscape and fire.
  • Dancer tenses: “Charmcasters. I should have known.”
  • Han perks with curiosity, fear, and excitement
  • has flashback of his mom warning him about wizards: “Stay away from wizards. You don’t want to be noticed by such as them. Get too close, and you might get burnt alive or turned into something foul and unholy. Common folk are like dirt under their feet.”
  • As the riders near their hiding spot, Dancer jumps out, spooking the horses
  • Dancer, loud and confident, tall with hands fisted at each side (speaks in Common tongue): “I am Fire Dancer of Marisa Pines Camp. This camp demands to know who you are and what wizards are doing on Hanalea, as is forbidden by the Nǽming.”
  • Han emerges reluctantly to stand beside his friend
  • Lead rider glares at them, then flinches in surprise when he sees Dancer before composing himself in a cool, relaxed way
  • Han looks confused about the reactions and thinks “Does he know Dancer? But… Dancer doesn’t seem to know him…”
  • The riders ignore Han as if he’s not even there
  • Dancer (speaks in Common tongue): “I asked your names.” Gestures towards the fire, “That looks like wizard flame to me.”
  • Lead rider (Micah) glances at other two riders, the others just sit in silence, looking off to the side.
  • Micah, cockily as if expecting them to cower when they hear his name: “I’m Micah Bayer, of Aerie House. We’re here on the queen’s orders. Queen Marianna and the Princesses Raisa and Mellony are hunting in the Vale below. We’re driving the deer down to meet them.”
  • Dancer, shaking his head in disbelief: “The queen ordered you to set fire to the mountain so she could have a good day’s hunting?”
  • Micah, dismissively: “I said so, didn’t I?”
  • Han squints at Micah suspiciously: “The deer don’t belong to the queen. We’ve as much right to hunt them as she does.”
  • Dancer, pointing at Micah’s amulet: “Anyway, you’re underage. You’re not allowed to use magic. Nor carry an amulet.”
  • Han looks surprised at this information and thinks: “How does Dancer know that?”
  • Micah glares at Dancer
  • Micah: “That’s wizard business. And no concern of yours.”
  • Dancer: “Well, Micah jinxflinger, if Queen Marianna wants to hunt deer in summer, she can come up into the high country after them. As she always has.”
  • Micah raises his eyebrows
  • Micah, snorting: “Where she can sleep on a dirt floor shoulder to shoulder with a dozen filthy kinsmen and go a week without a hot bath and come home stinking of wood smoke and sweat with a case of the night itches?” He and his friends laugh. “I don’t blame her for preferring the accommodations in the Vale.”
  • Han thinks: “Wow, he doesn’t know anything,” and remembers cozy lodges and sleeping benches and singing and storytelling around the campfire.
  • Dancer thrusts his chin out stubbornly (which I guess means raising his nose?)
  • Dancer: “Princess Raisa was fostered at Demonai Camp for three years.”
  • Micah: “the princess’s clan-bred father has some archaic ideas.” He and his friends laugh again. “Me, I wouldn’t want to marry a girl who’d spent time in the camps. I’d be afraid she’d been ruined.”
  • Dancer draws his knife in a flash
  • Dancer, his voice cold: “Repeat that, jinxflinger?
  • Micah jerks on his reins, trying to get his horse to step back from Dancer.
  • Dancer: “I’d say women have more to fear from jinxflingers than from anyone in the camps.”
  • Han, looking a bit nervous and worried, steps up beside Dancer, putting his hand on his own knife.
  • Micah’s eyes fix on Dancer’s knife
  • Micah: “Relax, copperhead.” there’s a pause. “Here’s the thing. My father says the girls who go to the camps come back proud and opinionated and difficult to manage. That’s all.” Micah smirks as if he’s told some funny joke.
  • Dancer is stone-faced: “Are you saying that the blooded heir to the throne of the Fells needs to be… managed?”
  • Han: “Dancer.” But Dancer dismisses him with a shake of his head
  • Han is already formulating a plan to win the fight if one does break out: Cut their cinch straps to drop them off their horses, get in close and kill Micah, the other’s will run.
  • One of the other riders (the Mander brothers) clears their throat nervously.
  • the oldest Mander brother grips his reins tightly (speaks in Vale dialect): “Micah, come on. Let’s go. We’ll miss the hunt.”
  • Micah, staring down at Dancer with a dark look: “Hold on, Miphis.” He stares some more at Dancer. (speaks in Common tongue) “Aren’t you called Hayden? It’s just… Hayden, isn’t it? A mongrel name, since you have no father.”
  • Dancer stiffens: “That is my Vale name.” He lifts his chin defiantly. “My real name is Fire Dancer.”
  • Micah, fingering the amulet: “Hayden is a wizard’s name. How dare you presume—”
  • Dancer: “I presume nothing. I didn’t choose it. I am clan. Why would I choose a jinxflinger name?”
  • Han, thinking to himself: “Good question, but why does someone like Micah know Dancer’s Vale name?”
  • Micah flushes red
  • Micah, drawling: “So you claim, Hayden. Maybe you fathered yourself. Which means you and your mother—”
  • Dancer throws his knife in a blur of speed, but Han manages to knock his arm aside just in time. the knife hits a tree instead.
  • Han hunches his shoulders while Dancer glares at him, thinking to himself: “Come on Dancer, Killing wizard friends of the queen would buy us a world of trouble.”
  • Micah sits on his horse, frozen with disbelief, then blanches white with anger.
  • he grabs his amulet, directs his free hand toward Dancer, and begins to mutter under his breath a charm.
  • Arkeda: “Micah.” he nidges his horse closer to Micah, “No. It’s not worth it. The fire was one thing. If they find out we—”
  • Micah: “Shut up, Arkeda. I’m going to teach  this base-born copperhead respect.” Irritated about his charm being interrupted, he starts over.
  • Han unslings his bow, notches an arrow, and aims it at Micah’s chest.
  • Han: “Hey, Micah. How about this? Shut it or I shoot.”
  • Micah squints at Han, as if surprised to see him there.
  • Miphis and Arkeda grad the hilts of their swords
  • After a tense pause, Micah lets go of his amulet, raising his hands.
  • Han: “Smart move. I’m guessing jinxes are slower than arrows.”
  • Micah, incredulously: “You tried to murder me.”
  • Micah: “Do you realize who I am? My father is High Wizard, counselor to the queen. When he finds out what you did…”
  • Dancer: “Why don’t you run back to Grey Lady and tell him all about it?” He jerks his head downslope, “Go on. You don’t belong here. Get off the mountain. Now.
  • Micah, softly, fingering his amulet: “Just remember, it’s a long way down the mountain. Anything can happen along the way.”
  • Han realizes that they could be ambushed by the wizards anytime.
  • Han: “You. Take off your jinxpiece. throw it on the ground.”
  • Micah: “This?”
  • Han nods, then Micah shakes his head.
  • Micah: “You can’t be serious.” Closing his fist around the amulet and snarling, “Do you know what this is?”
  • Han: “I have an idea. Take it off and throw it down.”
  • Bayer’s face turns pale and he freezes again in disbelief.
  • Micah: “You can’t use this, you know. If you even touch it, you’ll be incinerated.”
  • Dancer: “We’ll take our chances.”
  • Micah: “You’re nothing more than thieves, then. I should have known.”
  • Han: “Use your head. What would I do with truck like that? I just don’t want to have to be looking over my shoulder all the way home.”
  • Arkeda, leaning towards Micah (said in Valespeech): “Better give it to him. You know what they say about copperheads. They’ll cut your throat and drink your blood and feed you to their wolves so no one will ever find your bones.”
  • Miphis, nodding vigorously: “Or they’ll use us in rituals. They’ll burn us alive. Sacrifice us to their goddesses.”
  • Han clenches his jaw, but looks surprised and amused.
  • Micah: “I can’t give it to them, you idiot. You know why. If my father finds out I took it, we’ll all be punished.”
  • Akeda, muttering: “I told you not to take it. I told you it was a bad idea. Just because you want to impress Princess Raisa…”
  • Micah: “You know I wouldn’t have taken it if we were allowed to have our own. It was the only one I…” In a demanding voice, What are you looking at?” Just realizing that Han and Dancer might understand Valespeech.
  • Han: “I’m looking at someone who’s already in trouble and getting in deeper. Now, drop the amulet.”
  • Micah glares more intensely at Han than before.
  • Micah: “You’re not even clan. Who are you?”
  • Han: “... They call me Shiv, streetlord of Southbridge.”
  • Micah: “Shiv, you say? It’s strange… There’s something… You seem…”
  • Han, sweating: “I’ll count to five. then I put an arrow through your neck. One.”
  • Micah viciously yanks the amulet from his neck and throws it to the ground.
  • Micah: “Just try to pick it up. I dare you.”
  • Han looks nervously at the amulet.
  • Dancer: “Go on! Get out of here! I reckon you’d better think about how you’re going to put that fire out. If you don’t, I guarantee the queen won’t be happy, whether she asked you to start it or not.”
  • for a moment the riders and duo glare at each other, then the riders ride away hastily
  • Arkeda, turning back: “you fools! How is he supposed to put it out without the amulet?”
  • Dancer: “I hope he breaks his neck.”
  • Han sighs with relief, lowering his bow.
  • Han: “What was all that about your Vale name? Have you met Bayer before?”
  • Dancer, irritably: “Where would I meet a jinxflinger?”
  • Han: “Why did he say what he did about your father? How does he know that…”
  • Dancer, furiously: “How should I know? Forget about it. Let’s go.”
  • Han: “... What about this thing?” Points to the amulet on the ground. “Do you think he was bluffing?”
  • Dancer, from a safe distance, watches Han squat down and study the amulet.
  • Han: “I mean, do you think they need this thing to put the fire out?”
  • Dancer: “Just leave it. Let’s get out of here.”
  • Han: “That jinxflinger didn’t want to give this up. Must be valuable.” thinks to himself, “But you’re not a thief. Not anymore, remember?”
  • Han lifts the amulet by its chain with a stick. It spins hypnotically and reflects the sunlight
  • Dancer, disapprovingly: “What are you doing with that?”
  • Han, shrugging: “I don’t know.”
  • Dancer: “You should pitch it into the ravine. If Bayer took the thing without permission, let him explain what happened to it.”
  • Han wraps the amulet in a square of leather instead: “This doesn’t seem like the kind of thing you’d want to leave lying around for somebody to find.”
  • Dancer shrugs.
  • Han sighs and puts the wrapped up amulet into his satchel.
