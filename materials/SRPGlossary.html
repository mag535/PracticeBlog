Glossary

The clans:

An ethnic group native to the Fells that lives in the mountains of the region.  They have four groups who live in Camps: Demonai, Marisa Pines, Way, and Hunter’s. They live more simply than their Vale peers and teach their children skills such as wilderness survival and maintenance, crafting, trading,  storytelling, and many others. This culminates in the children choosing an apprenticeship on their 16th Name Day.

The clan camps celebrate the Name Days of their children together, usually at Marisa Pines Camp, with one celebration being held on the Summer Solstice for children born in the warm months, and another being held on the Winter Solstice for children born in the cold months.

They typically have bronze skin, black hair and brown eyes, but are known to take in orphans from the Vale. They also have the ability of Green magic.

The clan, just like wizard and the Flatlander army of the Fells, are an important military and spiritual power to the Gray Wolf throne. However, they are constantly in conflict with charmcaster (aka wizards) and sometimes even the Valefolk, who have internalized negative myths and stereotypes most likely started by wizards.

Name Day:

Someone’s birthday. In other words, it’s the day that person was named.

Green Magic:

It is unclear how Green magic works, but it manifests itself in the ability to heal, as in Willo Watersong, or the ability to create magical items such as talismans and amulets, such as the Demonai. Another character, Fire Dancer, managed to combine Green and High magic to create a cover of fog, something those with only Green magic can’t do and those with only High magic would end up driving themselves to near death to accomplish. Only people with clan blood have Green magic.

The Dyrnnewater River:

        A large, wide, ice-cold river that flows down Mt. Grey Lady in the east, through the Vale, into the Shivering Fens in the west.

        It holds significant spiritual and religious importance to the clan, but has been polluted by refugees and homeless people in the Vale emptying their slop jars into it.

Burnt Tree Meadow:

         A meadow on Mt. Hanalea that was the site of a wildfire a couple years prior to the start of the first book. It is now filled with charred fallen lodgepole trees and stumps, tall grass, wildflowers, knee-high pine trees . blackberry and other bramble bushes, and is brightly lit by the sun.

High Magic:

         Only those with wizard blood can use High magic. It consists of incantations and magic items. However, the wizard must have an amulet, only made by the Demonai, to channel or store the magical energy they naturally create. However, just like any other energy, it has to be replenished in order to cast more spells and the wizard that over taxes their energy supply may end up dead.
